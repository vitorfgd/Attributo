    Attributo: Pro License Manager – User Manual

    Thank you for choosing Attributo. This tool is designed to automate the tedious process of asset attribution and legal compliance within Unity. By documenting your licenses at the moment of import, you ensure your project is always "audit-ready."

    1. Quick Start

    First-Time Setup

    1. Import the Package: Once imported, Attributo is active. You don't need to toggle any settings.
    2. The Licenses Folder: It is recommended to keep all your LicenseData assets in a central folder (e.g., Assets/Licenses).

    The Import Workflow

    When you drag a new asset (model, texture, audio, etc.) into your Assets folder:

    1. Automatic Detection: Attributo checks if the folder (or its parents) already has a license.
    2. The Wizard: If no license is found, a window will appear.
      • Select: Choose an existing license from your library.
      • Create: Quickly generate a new license template.
      • Mute: If the asset is your own work, click "Skip (Mute folder)" to create an empty link and stop future prompts for that directory.

    2. Managing Licenses (LicenseData)

    LicenseData assets are the heart of the system. They store the legal "identity" of an asset.

    Using the SPDX Fetcher

    1. Create a new LicenseData asset (Create > Attributo > License).
    2. Enter the SPDX Identifier (e.g., MIT, Apache-2.0, CC-BY-4.0).
    3. Click "Fetch Official SPDX Text" in the Inspector.
    4. Attributo will download the official legal text and populate the license field.

    Unique Attributions

    If you have two different assets that both use the MIT license but require different author credits:

    • Create two separate LicenseData assets.
    • Name one MIT_Envato_Skybox and the other MIT_GitHub_InventorySystem.
    • Use the Notes field to store specific "Copyright (c) Year Author" lines required by the license.

    3. The License Hub

    Access the central dashboard via Window > License Manager > License Hub.

    Audit Tab

    • View every LicenseLink currently active in your project.
    • Identify "unlicensed" gaps in your project hierarchy.
    • Use the Ping and View buttons to locate specific assets or licenses.

    Export Tab

    • Export ThirdPartyNotices.txt / CREDITS.md: Compile every unique license and note into a single file.
    • Auto-Deduplication: If multiple folders use the same license asset, Attributo lists the legal text only once in the export.

    4. Troubleshooting & FAQ

    • Why didn't the window pop up? Attributo ignores native Unity files (e.g. .mat, .anim, .asset) and anything under Packages/ to reduce noise. Scripts (.cs) and asmdefs are also ignored.
    • Can I license a single file? Attributo is folder-based. Assigning a license to a folder covers everything inside it.
    • Does this work in builds? No. Attributo is Editor-only. It has no impact on game performance or build size.

    5. Legal Disclaimer

    IMPORTANT: Attributo is an organizational aid and does not constitute legal advice. You are solely responsible for ensuring that all assets are correctly attributed and that license terms are followed. Always manually review your exported attribution file before shipping your project.
